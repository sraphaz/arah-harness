Use cases

Where a repo-first harness earns its place

Eight situations where governance, persistence and coordination matter more than raw generation.

UC·01

New Repository

Governance structure from the first commit: domains, agents, autonomy and gates exist before the first feature does.

UC·02

Legacy Repository

Progressive discovery without rebuilding the project — the harness proposes domains from the structure that already exists.

UC·03

Monorepo

Different responsibilities for different areas and products: path-based choreography gives each territory its own agents, advisors and gates.

UC·04

Regulated Engineering

Specs, evidence, executable gates and human approval — an audit trail that exists because the workflow produces it, not as an afterthought.

UC·05

Multi-agent Engineering

Explicit coordination between operational and consultative agents: roles, boundaries and shared signals instead of overlapping sessions.

UC·06

Consulting

A common kernel with client-specific overlays: the same governance machinery, adapted per engagement without forking the practice.

UC·07

Product Platform

Shared policies across different products: one policy surface, many repositories, consistent gates and autonomy rules.

UC·08

Model Migration

Swap models and tools while context, procedures and governance stay in the repository — nothing to re-teach, nothing lost.

Your engineering context should belong to your repository, not to a model provider.
